Mixed Reality Concept

Aura

A mixed-reality museum experience that allows museum visitors to experience the stories of artifacts in an immersive space. Concept submitted for Microsoft's 2017 Design Expo.

Team members: Michael Frampton, Branden Keller, Maureen McLennon, Brian Orlando

Date — Winter 2017
Duration — Jan 2017 to Mar 2017
Instructor — Axel Roesler

Aura is a museum experience that utilizes mixed reality technology to provide contextual information to museum artifacts. Museums today—and especially science and natural history museums—already are building immersive spaces: from the Pacific Science Center’s butterfly house, to planetariums, to details such as “environmental” murals in museum spaces. My team felt that mixed reality technology could push museum engagement further by providing immersive experiences that augments a user’s understanding of that object.

Aura does this by putting users in mixed reality environments that are attached to real, physical artifacts. These mixed reality environments includes animated versions of artifacts, environments brought to life with scale and sound, and integration of interactive, collaborative components.

Using insights we gathered from observations and conversations with staff and patrons of museums in our community, we crafted a museum experience that focused on immersion and engagement while also keeping incorporating the collaborative, social elements that are crucial to a museum's success.
