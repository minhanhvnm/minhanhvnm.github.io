Mixed Reality Concept

Aura

Aura is a museum experience that utilizes mixed reality technology to provide contextual information to museum artifacts.

Museums today—and especially science and natural history museums—already are building immersive spaces: from the Pacific Science Center’s butterfly house, to planetariums, to details such as “environmental” murals in museum spaces. My team felt that mixed reality technology could push museum engagement further by providing immersive experiences that augments a user’s understanding of that object.

Team members: Michael Frampton, Branden Keller, Maureen McLennon, Brian Orlando

Date — Winter 2017
Duration — 10 weeks
Instructor — Axel Roesler

Key Moments

1 A mixed reality doorway invites visitors into Aura's imersive museum space.

2 Users see museum artifacts from different angles, at different sizes, and at many points in the artifact’s life.

3 Users opt-in to Aura's immersive features.

4 Users can share their experience with fellow visitors.

5 Docents can further engage museum visitors by connecting their explanations with animated holograms.

6 Users experience contextual cues (environment, sound, scale) directly related to the artifact at hand.

Research

We visited the Pacific Science Center in downtown Seattle to take a look at how museums today use the tools at their disposal to craft an engaging experience for their patrons. We took our own observations, as well as talked to museum staff and visitors.

Emphasis on Museum Staff Museum staff were a critical part in facilitating excitement over the museum content. The staff I interviewed discussed how they modified what they said to suite the visitor they were talking to.

Interactive Exhibits Engage The exhibits that received the most engagement were the ones that visitors could somehow play with.

Immersion is Memorable Museum staff noted many visitors were drawn to the Butterfly House, a totally immersive environmental space that facilitated meaningful engagement with the content of the exhibit.

Ideation + Telling the Story

In our ideation, we focused on augmenting the value of having a dedicated museum space, and drew upon the opportunities we saw for immersive environments in museums.

Our final concept elevates museum artifacts—something intrisic to museum spaces—by putting them in mixed reality immersive environments. We also considered how discussion and collaboration would work in this mixed reality space.

My team decided on initial design direction via group brainstormigng and discussion. During the production phase of our project, my contributions included cinematography, animation of the “play button” effect, and assembling of the first cut of our concept video.
