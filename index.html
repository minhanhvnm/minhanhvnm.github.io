About

I, Minh-Anh —

Hi, I’m Minh-Anh. I’m a graphic designer from Seattle, WA.

My design career humbly began in the fifth grade, when, at the age of 10, I taught myself HTML and promptly began making HTML layouts on virtual cat websites for virtual cat money.

After entering the University of Washington several years early via the highly competitive UW Robinson Center's Early Entrance Program, I am set to graduate in June 2017 with a degree in Visual Communication Design. My interest in design lies in its use as a tool for communication.

I spent summer of 2016 interning at Rice Creative, a design studio in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. I also worked remotely with a team of designers in Seattle as part of AIGA's Design For Good Changemaker Series to rebrand Northwest Youth Services, a non-profit organizing serving homeless youth in Skagit and Whatcom Counties.

Besides design, I‘m a big fan of podcasts, classical music, photography, disposable cameras, and taro bubble tea.
