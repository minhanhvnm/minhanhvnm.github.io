Minh-Anh Nguyen — Visual Communication Design

Information Design

On U.S. Immigration —

An informational website describing U.S. immigration policies with a focus on barriers in the immigration process.

Date — Winter 2016
Duration — 10 Weeks
Instructor — Karen Cheng

Link to Marvel prototype ↗

The challenge for this project was, over the course of 10 weeks, to tell a story about U.S. immigration using data in the form of a website.

Initial Data Visualizations

As I learned more about the immigration process, I realized that many facts about the barriers to immigration never show up in public discussions about immigration policy. I challenged myself to illuminate these details, and better equip those who engage in conversations on immigration.

Exploring visualizating the U.S. visa bulletin.

Here, I explored visualizing U.S. visa waiting list data.

Planning the Story

As I sketched out my story, I initially framed the different types of U.S. visas as a linear narrative, hoping to illustrate why prospective immigrants can’t “just get in line”.

Exploring different variations

As I explored different variations, I realized that the linear narrative didn’t sufficiently illustrate the idea of different types of visas. As a result, I explored many different variations of navigation models for my story.

Some of my explorations.

Final Design Decisions

I used a simple top-bar navigation in the end, due to its simplicity of use and implication of different “rooms”. Different colors for each “room” differentiate between each type of visa.
